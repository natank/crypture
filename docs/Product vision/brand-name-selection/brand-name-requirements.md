# Brand Name Requirements Gathering

## Executive Summary

This document establishes the comprehensive requirements for selecting a brand name that aligns with Crypture's strategic positioning as a privacy-first, no-login crypto portfolio tracker. The requirements are derived from our business plan analysis and target market segmentation to ensure the final brand name resonates with our core values and audience needs.

---

## 1. Brand Values & Core Principles

### 1.1 Primary Brand Values
| Value | Description | Naming Implications |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Privacy First** | User data belongs to users—we don't collect, store, or sell it | Names should evoke security, control, local storage |
| **Simplicity** | Complex features delivered through intuitive experiences | Names should be easy to understand, pronounce, remember |
| **Transparency** | Clear communication about what we do and don't do | Names should be straightforward, not misleading |
| **Independence** | No exchange affiliations, no custodial risk | Names should suggest autonomy, self-reliance |
| **Accessibility** | Tools that work for beginners and experts alike | Names should be welcoming, not intimidating |

### 1.2 Brand Personality Traits
- **Trustworthy:** Reliable, secure, dependable
- **Clear:** Simple, transparent, straightforward
- **Modern:** Tech-forward, innovative, current
- **Approachable:** Friendly, accessible, not overly technical
- **Independent:** Self-sufficient, autonomous, free

## 2. Target Audience Analysis

### 2.1 Segment 1: Beginners (45% of target market)
**Profile:** New investors, < 1 year experience, $500-$10K portfolios

**Needs & Expectations:**
- Clarity and simplicity in naming
- Names that suggest ease of use
- Avoid technical jargon or complexity
- Trust indicators for first-time users

**Naming Preferences:**
- Descriptive names (e.g., "ClearPortfolio", "SimpleTrack")
- Benefit-oriented names (e.g., "EasyCrypto", "ClearView")
- Familiar word combinations

**Messaging Alignment:** "Track your crypto clearly—no expertise required"

### 2.2 Segment 2: Privacy-Conscious (20% of target market)
**Profile:** Tech-savvy, 2-5+ years experience, $10K-$100K+ portfolios

**Needs & Expectations:**
- Names that emphasize privacy and control
- Technical credibility without being obscure
- Security and sovereignty indicators
- Independence from traditional finance

**Naming Preferences:**
- Privacy-focused names (e.g., "CryptoVault", "PrivateLedger")
- Security-oriented names (e.g., "Fortress", "Sentinel")
- Independence-themed names (e.g., "Sovereign", "Autonomi")

**Messaging Alignment:** "Your portfolio. Your data. Your control."

### 2.3 Segment 3: Power Users (35% of target market)
**Profile:** Active traders, 3+ years experience, $50K-$500K+ portfolios

**Needs & Expectations:**
- Professional, sophisticated naming
- Names suggesting advanced capabilities
- Intelligence and analytics indicators
- Efficiency and performance focus

**Naming Preferences:**
- Professional names (e.g., "CryptoAnalytics", "PortfolioPro")
- Intelligence-focused names (e.g., "Insight", "Quantum")
- Performance-oriented names (e.g., "Apex", "Vertex")

**Messaging Alignment:** "AI-powered insights for serious investors"

## 3. Naming Criteria & Requirements

### 3.1 Functional Requirements
| Criterion | Requirement | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Memorability** | Easy to recall after 1-2 exposures | Word-of-mouth growth, brand recall |
| **Pronunciation** | Simple phonetic structure, clear pronunciation | Accessibility, verbal communication |
| **Spelling** | Intuitive spelling, minimal ambiguity | Searchability, domain matching |
| **Length** | 2-3 syllables, 6-12 characters optimal | Memorability, domain availability |
| **Scalability** | Works across product lines and features | Future expansion possibilities |

### 3.2 Technical Requirements
| Requirement | Specification | Priority |
|-------------|----------------|----------|
| **Domain Availability** | .com, .io, .app domains available | Critical |
| **Trademark Clearance** | No conflicting trademarks in key markets | Critical |
| **Social Handles** | Available on Twitter, Discord, Reddit | High |
| **Search Engine** | Low competition for SEO terms | Medium |
| **International** | No negative meanings in major languages | High |

### 3.3 Brand Alignment Requirements
| Alignment Area | Requirement | Success Metric |
|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Privacy Positioning** | Name suggests security, control, or local storage | 80% of privacy segment associates with privacy |
| **Simplicity Promise** | Name feels approachable, not overly technical | 90% of beginners find name unintimidating |
| **Trust Building** | Name sounds reliable, established, professional | 85% associate with trustworthiness |
| **Differentiation** | Clearly distinct from CoinTracker, CoinStats, Blockfolio | 70% recall distinct from competitors |

## 4. Competitive Landscape Analysis

### 4.1 Competitor Naming Patterns
| Competitor | Name Type | Pattern | Weakness |
|------------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **CoinTracker** | Descriptive | [Asset] + [Function] | Generic, overused |
| **CoinStats** | Descriptive | [Asset] + [Data] | Technical, cold |
| **Blockfolio** | Compound | [Technology] + [Function] | Associated with failed exchange |
| **Delta** | Abstract | Single concept | Financial services connotation |

### 4.2 Naming Opportunities
**Gaps in Competitive Landscape:**
- Privacy-focused naming (no competitor emphasizes privacy)
- Simplicity-focused naming (all competitors are complex)
- Trust/security positioning (post-breach opportunity)
- No-login/instant access concepts

**Naming Approaches to Consider:**
1. **Privacy-First:** Emphasize data sovereignty and security
2. **Simplicity-First:** Highlight ease of use and clarity
3. **Trust-First:** Focus on reliability and independence
4. **Hybrid:** Combine multiple value propositions

### 4.3 Competitive Differentiation Requirements
- **Avoid:** "Coin", "Track", "Stats", "Portfolio" overuse
- **Emphasize:** Privacy, clarity, simplicity, trust
- **Position:** As the privacy-first alternative
- **Communicate:** Instant access without accounts

## 5. Strategic Positioning Requirements

**Naming Implications:**
- Must support "privacy" and "simplicity" dual positioning
- Should suggest "control" and "independence"

**Primary Brand Name Requirements:**

- Scalable to potential sub-brands (Mobile, Pro, API)
- Suitable for app store naming conventions
- Translates well to logo and visual identity

**Future-Proofing Considerations:**
- Accommodates potential product line expansion
- Works for B2B and B2C contexts
- Suitable for international markets
- Flexible enough for brand evolution

## 6. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### 6.1 Trademark Considerations
**Search Requirements:**
- USPTO database clearance
- EU trademark database clearance
- International class 9 (software) and 36 (financial services)
- Common law trademark search
- Domain name trademark conflicts

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Avoid descriptive terms that can't be trademarked
- Steer clear of existing financial service marks
- Consider geographic variations in trademark law
- Plan for international trademark protection

### 6.2 Regulatory Compliance
**Financial Services Considerations:**
- Avoid names implying banking, investment advice, or custody
- Steer clear of terms suggesting guaranteed returns
- Consider regulatory perceptions in key markets
- Ensure name doesn't trigger financial licensing requirements

**Data Privacy Implications:**
- Name should align with privacy-by-design positioning
- Avoid terms suggesting data collection or analysis
- Consider GDPR and privacy regulation perceptions

### 7.3 Emotional Response Requirements
**Target Emotional Associations:**
- Trust: 85% of respondents should feel trustworthy
- Clarity: 80% should associate with simplicity
